# IDEAS Storyline

* New Player Hook
  * Players begin as a newly cloned citizen at Furlong Station — a lively frontier hub where newcomers find their feet, meet other settlers, and discover the possibilities of a life in space.
  * The human race is spreading across a rough frontier, and Furlong Station is a relatively safe launchpad for anyone ready to explore what lies beyond.
  * The player's first steps are about discovery, identity, and choosing their own path — from socialising and trading to venturing out into uncharted space.

* Setting
  * A rough, practical space-faring society built from salvage, improvisation, and necessity.
  * The frontier stretches out beyond Furlong Station: new stations to settle, distant planets to reach, supply lines to establish, and communities to build.
  * The world feels battered, lived-in, and full of opportunity — dangerous at the edges but vibrant with life wherever people have taken root.

* Character Identity
  * The player is a clone-born citizen, part of a new generation shaping what the future of humanity looks like.
  * Their existence raises questions about personal worth, memory, and what it means to be human.
  * Other settlers may treat the player with curiosity, suspicion, or hope depending on their role.

* Early Game Experience
  * Furlong Station is a welcoming home base: players can hang out, chat, and explore without ever engaging with survival mechanics if they do not want to.
  * Optional tasks — station maintenance, food production, repairs, simple trade runs — teach the basics for players who want to go deeper.
  * Small missions introduce movement, crafting, resource gathering, and cooperation at the player's own pace.
  * New clones begin in a shared dormitory — bunk beds, communal space, no privacy — reflecting their low standing as unproven citizens.
  * Furlong Station's development ceiling and high capsule rent are designed to gently push capable players toward private stations and the excitement of the wider frontier.

* Civilization and Survival
  * The colony must build outposts, secure supply lines, and defend fragile settlements from scarcity and danger.
  * Survival is not just about combat; it is also about medicine, shelter, fuel, and community.
  * The player's actions can help shape whether the human race endures or fades — but at Furlong Station, there is always a place to rest and enjoy the company of others first.

* Themes
  * Hope in a harsh frontier.
  * The ethics of cloning, inheritance, and identity.
  * Building a future from scraps in a world that has already failed once.
  * The tension between personal freedom and the survival of the species.
