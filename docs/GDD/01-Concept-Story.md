# IDEAS Storyline

* New Player Hook
  * Players begin as a test-tube clone baby raised in a sterile nursery aboard a struggling frontier station.
  * The human race is on the edge of extinction, and the colony is trying to establish outposts to preserve civilization.
  * The player’s first steps are about survival, identity, and learning how the station works.

* Setting
  * A rough, practical space-faring society built from salvage, improvisation, and necessity.
  * Outposts are fragile lifelines placed across dangerous space to gather resources, grow food, and keep humanity alive.
  * The world feels battered, lived-in, and always one failure away from collapse.

* Character Identity
  * The player is a clone-born citizen, valuable because they are part of a new generation of human survival.
  * Their existence raises questions about personal worth, memory, and what it means to be human.
  * Other settlers may treat the player with curiosity, suspicion, or hope depending on their role.

* Early Game Experience
  * The player learns by helping with station maintenance, food production, repairs, and simple trade tasks.
  * Small missions teach the basics of movement, crafting, resource gathering, and cooperation.
  * Each success strengthens the station and gives the player a reason to keep growing.

* Civilization and Survival
  * The colony must build outposts, secure supply lines, and defend fragile settlements from scarcity and danger.
  * Survival is not just about combat; it is also about medicine, shelter, fuel, and community.
  * The player’s actions can help shape whether the human race endures or fades.

* Themes
  * Hope in a harsh frontier.
  * The ethics of cloning, inheritance, and identity.
  * Building a future from scraps in a world that has already failed once.
  * The tension between personal freedom and the survival of the species.
