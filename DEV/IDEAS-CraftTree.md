# IDEAS Craft Tree

* Material Input System
  * Items should be built from a small set of core materials such as metal, fuel, plastic, fabric, food, and rare salvaged components.
  * Each recipe should clearly list the required inputs, tools, and station facilities needed to produce an item.
  * Material quality and scarcity should affect the final output, durability, and usefulness of crafted goods.

* Crafting Categories
  * Basic Tools
    * Hammers, cutters, patch kits, portable lights, and simple repair devices.
  * Furniture and Habitat
    * Beds, chairs, storage, walls, workbenches, and small room modules.
  * Ship and Station Parts
    * Engines, thrusters, generators, docking hardware, and maintenance kits.
  * Consumables
    * Food packs, medicine, fuel cells, coolant, and survival supplies.
  * Specialized Goods
    * Trade cargo, custom machines, and luxury or industrial items for colony growth.

* Crafting Flow
  * Players gather raw materials, process them at a workbench or machine shop, and assemble final items.
  * Some crafts require multiple stages, such as refining ore, fabricating parts, and then assembling a complete device.
  * Time, energy, and station capacity should all matter when making valuable items.

* Progression and Quality
  * Better tools and higher skill ranks improve crafting yield, reduce waste, and unlock advanced recipes.
  * Rare materials can create superior versions of items with better stats or longer lifespan.
  * Crafting should support both practical survival needs and trade-driven economy goals.

* Economy Integration
  * Crafted items can be sold, traded, leased, or used to upgrade stations and outposts.
  * Players should be able to specialize in producing high-demand goods for the colony economy.
  * Crafting should connect naturally to the tech tree, markets, and station ownership systems.
